About

I am a first-year chemical engineering technology student interested in how engineering principles apply to real-world systems. Outside of coursework, I enjoy fishkeeping, which has given me a practical understanding of how delicate chemical balances are in aquatic environments.

This perspective has shaped my interest in developing processes that reduce environmental impact. I'm particularly interested in developing and improving large scale systems that may reduce overall pollutants and the demand for large-scale remediation.

Notes, Experiments & a bit of Chaos

A running log of what I’m learning, testing, and figuring out along the way.

March 2026 – Chem 2

Worked on an chemistry II assignment to practice anyaltical skills. I prepared cola samples and used a spectrophotometer to determine the phosphate concentration. This allowed for more practice creating and using standard curves with absorbance data to calculate unknown concentrations.

Reflections: I feel comfortable with this process, my Excel skills have improved a lot too, so inputting the data for analysis is actually pretty fun now.

Feb 2026 – Water Treatment

Tested water samples and used a coagulating agent to remove suspended particles, demonstrating how chemical treatment can improve water clarity.

Reflections: It was interesting to see how the particles began to clump together and then settle to the bottom of the beakers. In general, I've become more aware of water quality and what it takes to make sure we have clean water.

Feb 2026 – Interesting dream

I had a dream about electron clouds and orbitals existing in different planes in 3D space. It almost makes sense. Trying to visualize these concepts clearly is one thing, but apparently my brain decided to keep working on it while I was asleep.

Chemistry is officially living rent-free in my head now.

Feb 2026 – Organic Chemistry

Performed reflux reactions and recrystallization to purify compounds. Measured melting points using a Mel Temp apparatus to assess purity. This helped me understand how purification techniques affect yield and product quality.

Reflections: It was a fun lab, The most difficult part was trying to monitor the melting point apparatus while everyone around me decided it was the perfect time to become comedians. I stayed focused and got it done though. Now that I've purified the acetaminophen from the crude substance, I may actually need it.

feb 2026 – Separation Methods

Conducted liquid–liquid extraction to isolate caffeine from tea. Learned how phase separation works and how solvent choice impacts extraction efficiency. Also reinforced proper solvent handling and safety practices.

Reflections: I’m still figuring out how I would choose alternative solvents on my own to do seperations, particularly when it comes to considering the differences in boiling points.

Jan 2026 – Colorimetry

In this lab, we used a colorimeter to observe different wavelengths of light. The overhead lights were turned off to make the signal easier to see, which was supposed to help with alignment and improve accuracy.

I thought my readings were properly aligned with the crosshair, but my data ended up being inconsistent. After reviewing what went wrong, I realized that I had slightly moved the ring, which threw off the alignment and affected my measurements.

This made me understand how sensitive these instruments are and how even small setup errors can significantly impact results.

Lesson Learned: Just a little too blind to play in the dark, precision and alignment matter more than I thought.

Ongoing development of Lab Skills

Gaining experience with titrations, pH measurements, and solution preparation. Practicing accurate dilutions and recording experimental data clearly in lab notebooks. Focused on improving precision and consistency in experimental work.

Reflections 2025: I have to say, titrations have been a true test of my sanity...and possibly my saintliness as well. How does one drop speed-run you from clear to the darkest blue/purple? Well, I’m off to go listen to Indigo Nxre to recover.

Update: March 2026 - Not shooting drastically past the endpoint on titrations anymore. I blame the burette tips for past faults :)
